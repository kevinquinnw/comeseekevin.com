The Future of AI in Cars

Will it ever happen? - 2020/03/01

How close are we to fully-autonomous cars?

Well it's been done. If you haven't heard of Comma AI, you should look at George Hortz's work. It's incredible, he is so passionate about it that he's live-streamed his open source software on the highway, operating the car.

It works but I think it's going to get shelved due to an ongoing ethical debate.

Being a car enthusiast, I feel as if I'm on the opposite side of the spectrum. It seems as if a lot of car enthusiasts seem to dislike autonomous cars, due to the fact that a human isn't in control of the car. I feel like to have some guidance with artificial intelligence can make the roads a lot safer for everybody.

With artificial intelligence, it will help drivers make decisions in dangerous situations where you have to act quick. That's why this idea is so cool. Automating the decision will definitely help relieve drivers of panic and distress. There is one problem though.

How does the car decide what life to prioritize?

The driver or the pedestrian?

Well here are three universal preferences that I've gathered:

  • to spare human lives over animals
  • save more lives over fewer
  • priortize young people over old ones

And that's the problem. Trying to implement some universal code of ethics is going to be very difficult. The influence autonomous vehicles will have on policy and the legal bind it could create will be controversial to say the least.
